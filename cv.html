I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Theoretical Computer Science at University of Michigan (Ann Arbor), fortunate to be co-advised by Nikhil Bansal and Euiwoong Lee. I obtained my Dual Degree (BTech+MTech) from Computer Science and Engineering Department, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur. I'm interested in Theoretical CS, including Approximation, Online, Randomized, Parameterized Algorithms, and Game Theory.

Over the past years, I have had great collaborators including Prof. Ashish Chiplunkar (IIT Delhi), Arindam Khan (IISc Bangalore), Andreas Wiese (UofChile), and Palash Dey (IIT Kharagpur).

On Guillotine Separable Packings for the 2D Geometric Knapsack Problem

Arindam Khan, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma, Andreas Wiese
InSoCG 2021, Contributed Talk HALG 2021.

PPTAS (Pseudo-Polynomial Time Approximation Scheme) for the 2D Knapsack problem in a Guillotine Separable setting, improving the best approximation factor of 3.

Two Dimensional Guillotine Strip Packing

Arindam Khan, Aditya Lonkar, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma, Andreas Wiese
In ICALP 2022.

A (3/2 + ε)-approximation algorithm for 2D Strip Packing in Guillotine Separable setting, including a PPTAS.

On Parameterized Complexity of Liquid Democracy

Palash Dey, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma
In CALDAM 2021.

Parameterized algorithms for Liquid Democracy — a voting system where voters may delegate their votes.

Network Design for Binary Networked Public Good Games

Palash Dey, Amatya Sharma

Analysis of PSNE in game-theoretic models; established hardness and XP algorithms.
