I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Theoretical Computer Science at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, fortunate to be co-advised by Nikhil Bansal and Euiwoong Lee. I earned a Dual Degree (BTech+MTech) from the Computer Science and Engineering Department, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur. My research intersts include Theoretical CS, including Approximation, Online, Randomized, Parameterized Algorithms, and Game Theory. Lately, I have been delving into Approximation algorithms and Constrained Satisfiability Problems.

Over the past years, I have had great collaborators including Prof. Ashish Chiplunkar (IIT Delhi), Arindam Khan (IISc Bangalore), Andreas Wiese (UofChile), and Palash Dey (IIT Kharagpur).

Publications

My research spans Approximation, Online, Randomized, Parameterized Algorithms, Algorithmic Game Theory. My prior research focuses on the design and analysis of Approximation and Parameterized Algorithms for Computational Geometric and Game Theoretic Problems. (Note: For theory papers, author names are listed alphabetically.)

Min CSPs on Complete Instances

Aditya Anand, Euiwoong Lee, Amatya Sharma
In SODA 2025

Initiated the study of complete instances for Minimization Constraint Satisfiability Problems. Proved results for Min-2CSP and k-CSPs.

A Decomposition Approach to Weighted k-server problem

Nikhil Ayyadevara, Ashish Chiplunkar, Amatya Sharma
In FSTTCS 2024.

Presented a decomposition approach for online weighted k-server.

On Guillotine Separable Packings for the 2D Geometric Knapsack Problem

Arindam Khan, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma, Andreas Wiese
InSoCG 2021, Contributed Talk HALG 2021.

PPTAS (Pseudo-Polynomial Time Approximation Scheme) for the 2D Knapsack problem in a Guillotine Separable setting, improving the best approximation factor of 3.

Two Dimensional Guillotine Strip Packing

Arindam Khan, Aditya Lonkar, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma, Andreas Wiese
In ICALP 2022.

A (3/2 + ε)-approximation algorithm for 2D Strip Packing in Guillotine Separable setting, including a PPTAS.

On Parameterized Complexity of Liquid Democracy

Palash Dey, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma
In CALDAM 2021.

Parameterized algorithms for Liquid Democracy — a voting system where voters may delegate their votes.

Network Design for Binary Networked Public Good Games

Palash Dey, Amatya Sharma

Analysis of PSNE in game-theoretic models; established hardness and XP algorithms.
